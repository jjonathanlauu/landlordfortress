About the Book

In a tranquil Swiss villa, two titans met: Romain Rolland, the moral conscience of a war‑torn Europe, and Mahatma Gandhi, the architect of nonviolent revolution. Their communion sparked a vision for a new humanity. This book traces Rolland’s lifelong quest for a heroism strong enough to mend a broken world—his secular faith in art, his independence of spirit during the Great War, his epic Jean‑Christophe, and his ideals tested against the rise of fascism. Finally, it follows the “eternal echo” of his spirit into a new cultural context, revealing a heroism that transcends borders and time.
